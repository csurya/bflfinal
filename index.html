What is Processing and why learn it?Processing is a programming language and development environment that promotes software literacy within the visual arts and visual literacy within technology. If you don’t know programming, it’s a great language to start with: it’s visual, fun, there’s excellent documentation (online + several books), and there’s a vibrant online community of people asking and answering questions. If you do know programming, it’s great for image processing, data visualization, interactive programs, and open GL integration, and more.

About this siteThese videos were made by Daniel Shiffman for ICM (Introduction to Computational Media), a class that teaches Processing to first-year graduate students at NYU’s ITP, an interdisciplinary arts and technology program sometimes called “the center for the recently possible.”
The videos also loosely follows his book Learning Processing.
